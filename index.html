Florida has no mandatory waiting period. Uncontested divorces typically finalize in 3–6 months. Contested cases can take 1–2 years depending on complexity. We work to resolve your case as efficiently as possible without sacrificing your outcome.

Florida follows "equitable distribution" — marital assets and debts are divided fairly, not necessarily 50/50. Courts weigh each spouse's financial situation, contributions to the marriage, length of the marriage, and post-divorce economic circumstances.

Florida courts determine time-sharing based solely on the child's best interests — considering each parent's ability to foster the child's relationship with the other parent, home stability, school and community ties, work schedules, and the child's own preferences when age-appropriate.

Yes. Florida's 2023 alimony reforms eliminated permanent alimony. Courts now award bridge-the-gap, rehabilitative, or durational alimony based on the length of the marriage and the parties' financial circumstances.

Yes. Florida allows modification when there has been a "substantial change in circumstances" — a significant income change, relocation, remarriage, or change in the child's needs.
